What is Investing?

Investing is the process of putting your money to work to generate returns over time. This can include buying assets like stocks, bonds, real estate, or mutual funds, with the goal of building wealth or generating income.

Why Invest?

  • Grow your money over time
  • Beat inflation
  • Achieve financial goals (e.g. retirement, education)
  • Build passive income streams

Common Investment Vehicles

  • Stocks: Ownership in companies with potential for growth or dividends.
  • Bonds: Loans to companies or governments with fixed interest returns.
  • Mutual Funds: Professionally managed portfolios pooling money from many investors.
  • ETFs: Funds that trade like stocks and offer built-in diversification.
  • Real Estate: Investing in property for rental income or resale gains.
  • Cash Equivalents: Low-risk, low-return assets like CDs and money markets.

Basic Investment Principles

  • Start early: Compounding works best over time.
  • Diversify: Don’t put all your eggs in one basket.
  • Be consistent: Regular contributions build wealth.
  • Understand risk: Higher potential returns usually come with higher risk.

How to Get Started

Step 1: Define Your Goals

Decide what you want to achieve: retirement savings, wealth building, or income generation.

Step 2: Choose a Brokerage

Pick a platform that offers low fees, good tools, and an easy-to-use interface.

Step 3: Select Your Investments

Start with diversified funds or ETFs if you're unsure where to begin.

Step 4: Monitor and Adjust

Track performance and rebalance your portfolio regularly based on your goals.

Beginner Mistakes to Avoid

  • Timing the market: Focus on time *in* the market, not perfect timing.
  • Ignoring fees: Hidden costs can erode returns over time.
  • Lack of research: Know what you’re investing in and why.
  • Overreacting to volatility: Stay the course during market ups and downs.
